# MATIC Robot — Reverse Engineering & Component Analysis

**Kovari Industries** — San Francisco, CA
**Date:** April 2–5, 2026

---

## Overview

During a 72-hour on-site engineering trial at Kovari Industries, a pre-seed hospitality robotics startup, I performed a component-level reverse engineering analysis of a commercial MATIC robot platform. The goal was to build the team's institutional knowledge of the MATIC's electrical architecture and create a reusable reference for designing Kovari's own power and control systems around it.

## Approach

With a real shipping deadline one month out — a robot headed to a hotel in Houston — I had full autonomy to choose what to work on. I prioritized the MATIC teardown because the team lacked a structured component inventory, and every downstream board design (harness cleanup, wireless control, production power distribution) depended on understanding what was already in the robot.

The reverse engineering process involved:

- **Physical teardown and inspection** — Opened assemblies, traced wiring harnesses, and documented connector pinouts, wire gauges, and routing paths across the robot's power, control, and sensor subsystems.
- **Component identification** — Cross-referenced part markings, package types, and datasheets to catalog individual ICs, passives, connectors, and electromechanical components. Captured approximately 80% of the robot's critical components with part numbers and functional descriptions.
- **PCB traceability decoding** — Scanned and interpreted QR codes on MATIC PCBs (e.g., serial string `3CSNS43025802212`) to extract board revision identifiers, manufacturing date codes, and production line information.
- **Photo documentation** — Photographed and annotated key assemblies, board layouts, connector interfaces, and wiring runs to create a visual reference library for the team.

## Deliverables

- **Matic RE.xlsx** — Structured spreadsheet cataloging identified components with designators, part numbers, package types, and functional notes.
- **ImagesCollected.zip** — Archive of all photographs and written notes taken during the teardown, organized by subsystem.
